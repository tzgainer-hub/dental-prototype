First Touch — Drop the Link

Day 1

Character count ~220 chars · 2 SMS segments
Send time Tuesday–Thursday, 9–11am or 1–3pm local time. Never Monday morning or Friday afternoon.
No reply after 2–3 days
Text 2

Follow-Up — One Question Close

Day 4–5
9:41 AM●●●
[Your Name] Point Zero AI
Hey [First Name] — following up on the dental site I sent. One quick question: when a new patient Googles [Practice Name], does your current site make them want to call?

If not — that's exactly what we fix. $2,500 flat, done in 2 weeks. Happy to show you more.
Delivered
Why it works The "one quick question" pattern gets responses. It plants a doubt they already feel — most dentists know their site is outdated. The question makes them picture their own site being judged.
Price reveal Drop the $2,500 number here for the first time in text. It anchors them before a call. Objections surfacing over text are easier to handle than silence.
Character count ~240 chars · 2 SMS segments
Alternate shorter version "Hi [First Name] — did you get a chance to check out that dental site? Curious what you thought. One new patient pays for the whole thing." ~155 chars · 1 segment
Still no reply after 4–5 more days
Text 3

Last Touch — The Permission Text

Day 10–12
9:41 AM●●●
[Your Name] Point Zero AI
[First Name] — last text from me on this. Not a fit right now, totally fine — just reply "not now" and I'll check back in a few months. Otherwise, here's the site one more time:

dental-prototype-production.up.railway.app

Two weeks to a site your patients will notice. — [Your Name]
Delivered
Why it works The "permission to stop" text paradoxically gets the highest reply rate of the three. Giving them an easy out ("just say not now") feels low-pressure — and many prospects who ignored the first two will respond to this one.
Two outcomes both win They say "not now" → you have a confirmed future touchpoint. They don't reply or say "tell me more" → you follow up with a call. Either way, you know where you stand.
Character count ~280 chars · 2 SMS segments
After this Move to email-only for 60 days, then restart the SMS sequence. Never text more than 3 times in a 30-day window.
After they click the demo link / show interest
Post-Demo

Delivered
When to send this If you're using a link tracker (Bitly, etc.) and see a click, send this within the hour. If they replied with any interest ("looks good," "interesting," "send me more"), send immediately.
Goal Get them to name a time. Once they say "Thursday at 2" — they're committed. That call has a 60–70% close rate.
Character count ~195 chars · 2 SMS segments

Close Text

Send the Intake Form — Lock It In

Within 1 hour of verbal yes
9:41 AM●●●
[Your Name] Point Zero AI
Great talking, [First Name]! Sending over the intake form now — it's short, covers everything we need to build your site. Once we have it back + your logo/photos, we're building:

dental-prototype-production.up.railway.app/intake-form.html

The invoice will follow by email. Excited to build this for you!
Delivered
Why send immediately Momentum dies fast. Every hour between "yes" and the intake form increases the chance they cool off or a spouse/office manager talks them out of it. Send within the hour — ideally within minutes.
What this text does Gives them something concrete to do. "We're building" sets the expectation that this is happening. The invoice following by email makes it feel official.
If they don't fill out the form in 48 hours Text: "Hey [First Name] — just want to make sure you got the intake form link. Any questions on it? We're ready to start as soon as we hear back."

You Say"No worries at all — I'll take you off the list. Just one thing: if your website situation ever changes, the demo's always live at [link]. Good luck with the practice!"
They Say"Send me more info."
You Say"On it — sending details by email in the next 5 minutes. Meanwhile, the full demo is live at [link] — everything's there including pricing. What's your email?"
They Say"How much does it cost?"
You Say"Two options: $1,599 one-time + $149/mo hosting (1 revision round) OR $2,500 flat — that includes 12 months hosting + 3 revision rounds. Most practices go with the $2,500. Done in 2 weeks. Want to hop on a quick call?"
They Say"We already have a website."
You Say"Most practices do — and honestly a lot of them are losing patients without knowing it. Takes 2 min to see what we built: [link]. If yours already looks like that, you're in great shape and you don't need us. 😊"
They Say"Call me."
You Say"Calling now!" — then call immediately. Do not send another text. This is a hot lead.
