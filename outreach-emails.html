Closer Notes & Best Practices

Lead with the demo link, always

The site sells itself. Get eyes on it before you try to explain anything. "Just take 2 minutes and look at this link" is your best opener.

Who you're talking to matters

In small practices: reach the doctor directly. In larger practices: start with the office manager or practice administrator — they often control vendor decisions.

Push Option B ($2,500)

It's the better value and higher ticket. Lead with it. Only offer Option A if they push back on price. The extra $900 covers hosting year one + 2 more revision rounds.

Dentist egos are a feature, not a bug

Use words like: "premium," "custom," "your brand," "your reputation," "practices that take their online presence seriously." Avoid: "template," "basic," "affordable."

Two-week turnaround is a close

"Most agencies take 3–6 months. We're done in two weeks from the day we get your intake form." This is a real differentiator — use it.

Send the intake form on close

When they say yes, immediately send them the intake form link: /intake-form.html on the demo site. Build starts when they return it with their assets.

The "Google" angle always lands

"A new patient searches your practice name. What do they see? If your site looks like it was built in 2012, you just lost them." Most dentists feel this pain immediately.

No custom quotes, no negotiations

The price is the price. $1,599 or $2,500. Add-ons are listed separately. Holding the line makes the product feel premium and protects margins.
